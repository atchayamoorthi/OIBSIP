## 📖 Project Overview

This project is submitted as **Task 5** of the **Oasis Infobyte Cyber Security Internship**.

The objective of this task is to research **Social Engineering Attacks**, understand how attackers manipulate human psychology, study real-world attack examples, and learn the best prevention techniques used by organizations.

Unlike technical attacks that exploit software vulnerabilities, social engineering targets **human behavior**, making it one of the most dangerous cybersecurity threats.

# 📖 Conclusion

Social Engineering remains one of the most effective attack methods because it targets people rather than technology. By understanding common attack techniques such as phishing, pretexting, baiting, and quid pro quo, individuals and organizations can better recognize threats and reduce the risk of security breaches.

Regular awareness training, strong authentication, and verification procedures are essential to building a secure environment.
